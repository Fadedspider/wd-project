Explore Manuscript Libraries

Khuda Baksh Oriental Public Library, Patna

Close to the banks of the Ganges, in Patna, stands the Khuda Bakhsh Oriental Public Library. It opened to the public in October 1891 with a collection of 4,000 manuscripts. Today, it holds about 21,000 manuscripts and 2.5 lakh printed books. Declared an Institution of National Importance in 1969, it's a unique repository of Oriental literature.

Delhi Public Library

Started as a UNESCO project in 1951 and inaugurated by Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru, this library has grown into a premier public library system in Delhi. It operates through an extensive network of zonal libraries, branches, and mobile services across the city.

Thanjavur Maharaja Serfoji's Sarasvati Mahal Library

Located in Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu, this library is one of the oldest in Asia. It houses over 60,000 volumes, including a significant collection of Sanskrit manuscripts written in various scripts, focusing on literature, music, and medicine.

Rampur Raza Library

Founded in 1774 by Nawab Faizullah Khan, this library at Rampur is a treasure house of Indo-Islamic learning and art. It contains valuable manuscripts, historical documents, and Mughal miniature paintings.
